2 Trajectory Tracking

RMS Error1.27 mm % Error4.2% Max Error3.74 mm

The fingertip traces a 1:2 Lissajous figure in the flexion plane while a marker is tracked by camera. Integrating the position error over the complete path gives an RMS tracking error of 1.27 mm, which is about 4% of the 30 mm figure.

3 Sinusoidal Position Control

DC gain1.07 (+0.6 dB) −3 dB BW3.8 Hz

Closed-loop joint position-tracking frequency response from a position chirp. The controller tracks with near-unity gain up to ~3 Hz and a clean low-pass rolloff, with a −3 dB bandwidth of ~3.8 Hz. Runs 1 and 2 sweep from 0.1 to 30 Hz; run 3 sweeps from 30 to 50 Hz. Two independent repeat sweeps give the same gain curve, confirming linearity and repeatability.

Closed-loop tracking response of 0.1 to 30 Hz chirp sweep for runs 1 and 2, and 30 to 50 Hz for run 3.

Per-run time series and spectrograms; runs 1–2 sweep cleanly to ~30 Hz, run 3 command is corrupted due to aliasing.

4 Velocity Control Bandwidth

−3 dB BW> tested range gain~0.6 flat fitted corner37.6 Hz

Fingertip velocity-tracking response under a commanded velocity chirp. The velocity gain is essentially flat with no −3 dB rolloff in the tested band (encoder ~0.6 from 6–28 Hz; motion-capture flat over 1.8–6.8 Hz), so the velocity bandwidth exceeds the tested range; a first-order fit extrapolates a corner near 37.6 Hz.

5 Finger Cycle Time

Max Cycle Frequency3.45 Hz Range of Motion88.8°

Time to complete one full extension–flexion cycle through the entire range of motion, driven by a 3.5 Hz square wave. The finger tracks essentially perfectly: mean cycle time 0.290 s (3.45 Hz, within 1.5% of command) over 10 cycles, with the full ~89° MCP range reached every cycle.

7 Step Force Control

Settling0.2–0.3 s Rise Time35–70 ms Overshoot2–3%

Force step response tracking a square-wave force reference (alternating every ~1 s) at several force ranges, measured with a stationary load cell. Tests ran from force intervals starting at a low range of 1 N and stepping to 3 N, 10 N, 15 N, and 20 N. The high force range response is very fast and settles in ~0.2–0.3 s, whereas the low force range is fast but never fully settles.
